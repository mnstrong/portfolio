You set the box aside. You keep looking. The stack grows taller. The coffee grows colder. Years pass. The resumes blur together into one long, beige nightmare. You are still at your desk. You will always be at your desk. You never did find the right designer.

The End

← Go back. Open the box.

You decide to wing it. How hard can it be? Paper is paper. Aerodynamics is just, like, air stuff. You fold confidently — a crumple here, a crease there, something that vaguely resembles a triangle. You step outside and launch it with authority.

It veers immediately left. Then hard right. Then, defying every known law of physics, it curves in a wide arc — backwards. Directly at your face. Before you can move, it catches you square in the eye. Squared.

Your eye is on fire. Red. Weeping. You stand there in the parking lot, one hand over your face, your little crumpled triangle lying at your feet, victorious.

You never did get in touch with that designer. She was perfect for the role. But now you have a stabbed eye and a new, deeply personal fear of paper.

The End

← Try again (carefully this time)

When you pick up the rubber ducky, something doesn't feel right. It looks and feels like a regular duck, but the energy you get from it is all wrong. And has its eyes turned red? Before you can drop it, the duck's red eye turns and looks you straight in the eye. It starts to bulge and contort in your hand, its red eye slowly getting larger and larger. You can't stop staring at its glaring red eye as it gets too large for you to hold on to any longer. Its eye is huge now, its shape growing into a large round orb with what looks like sharp teeth. It looks at you hungrily. What are you waiting for, run!

The End
